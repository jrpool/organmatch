About OrganMatch

Your patient’s life depends on you—and on luck

You and your fellow surgeons have patients needing organ transplants. There are not enough organs to satisfy the need, so some patients will not survive the wait.

Organs and patients belong to various categories, based on genetics and body size. Not all organs are good matches for all patients.

Patients vary in their:

  • needs for a transplant
  • abilities to cooperate
  • post-transplant life expectancies
  • prospects for happy life
  • importances as parents and caregivers
  • expected contributions to social welfare

The inflow of new organs depends on unpredictable events, such as accidental deaths and consents to organ donation.

Whether your patients live or die will depend on them, on your skill in collegial decisionmaking, and on the luck of the draw.
